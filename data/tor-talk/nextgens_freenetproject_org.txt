Forget about MQV and HMQV ... they are flawed. Look at FHMQV ( or JFK(i|r) ( instead.
JFKi is what we use in Freenet ( In tor's case JFKr would probably make more sense though. But again, we had a different threat model and where trying to protect ourselves from DoS (memory and CPU). Before that we were using a signed-DH exchange checking for bad values like tor does at the moment and as far as I know that is still believed to be secure nowadays.
